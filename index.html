Who are we

We are a group of historical re-enactors based in County Kerry, Ireland.

We specialize in the Viking and Medieval periods, with all of our members coming from all different working professions and ages.

origins of our name

Our group's name is based around the beginnings of our founding member's re-enactment journey, who came to Ireland from Cornwall, which is in the southwest of England. A place with a very distinctive heritage and its own language.

In the Cornish language, the word cos translates to wooded place and branok translates to a crow or raven(is the same word for both). So with the combination of both words, it loosely translates to raven's wood.

Our aim

By studying historical resources, We can interpret how our ancestors lived, Also, it shows how different lifestyles and communities lived depending on the historical eras.

With this we can educate and entertain by bringing history to life. From our authentic living history camps, crafts and fighting displays, and much more.

Why do we do what we do

Within today's society, history is often portrayed in the media with TV series such as 'Vikings'. This has sparked the public imagination leading to people wanting to know more.

These little sparks can lead to a passion for a historical period, which can evolve into wanting the first-hand experience of what life was like for our ancestors.
